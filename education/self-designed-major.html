Cybersecurity (B.S.)

Self-Designed Major | Heidelberg University

This Cybersecurity program originated as a formally approved Self-Designed Major at Heidelberg University and is now serving as the pilot framework for a permanent Cybersecurity degree program.

The program was created to address a structural gap: while Heidelberg offered strong coursework in Computer Science, Data Analytics, and Business, it did not offer a degree capable of preparing students for modern cybersecurity roles. Rather than dilute cybersecurity into unrelated majors, this program was designed intentionally, with cybersecurity as the primary discipline, not a concentration or afterthought.

Program Purpose

To produce graduates who can:

  • Secure and administer modern networked systems
  • Identify, analyze, and mitigate vulnerabilities
  • Conduct penetration testing and adversarial analysis
  • Apply data analytics to security problems
  • Understand cyber operations within national, legal, and organizational contexts
  • Communicate technical findings professionally

Academic Requirements

  • 27+ dedicated major credits
  • 15+ credits at 300-400 level
  • Coursework spanning multiple academic disciplines
  • Required junior-year applied experience (SDN 370/371/372)
  • Required senior-year capstone and portfolio (SDN 400)

Why This Program Had to Be Created

Cybersecurity is not a subset of Computer Science. It is not an extension of Data Analytics. It is not equivalent to general Information Technology.

Cybersecurity is an operational, adversarial discipline. It requires fluency in systems, networks, threat behavior, defense mechanisms, and real-world constraints. Traditional majors fail here for predictable reasons:

  • Computer Science emphasizes algorithms, software development, and theory—not networks, attack surfaces, or defense
  • Data Analytics emphasizes statistics and visualization—not systems, exploitation, or security operations
  • IT-adjacent programs often stop at configuration and policy, without offensive or analytical depth

Choosing an existing major would have required significant external remediation—certifications, independent study, or an additional degree—to reach baseline cybersecurity competence. That defeats the purpose of a four-year program.

The Solution: Intentional Program Design

This program was not created for customization or convenience. It was created because no existing major could meet the requirements of cybersecurity education without compromise.

Creating this program ensured that cybersecurity was treated as the primary discipline—deliberately, rigorously, and with institutional accountability.

Institutional Impact

This program serves as the pilot framework for a permanent Cybersecurity degree at Heidelberg University. The pilot phase allows:

  • Curriculum testing across departments
  • Validation of course sequencing and outcomes
  • Capstone and internship feedback to inform permanent program design

The long-term objective is a fully named and standardized Cybersecurity (B.S.) program at Heidelberg University, built on this foundation.
